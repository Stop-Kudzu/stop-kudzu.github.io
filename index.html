Stopping The Kudzu Invasion

KUDZU IS DESTROYING YOUR TREES. WILL YOU STOP IT?

What is Kudzu?

Kudzu is a vine that originally came from Asia, specifically Eastern Asia (Japan, China, etc.), but settled well into many ecosystems here in the USA. It lives by grabbing onto a larger plant, and using it to gain sunlight. In doing so, the Kudzu eventually smothers its host tree, killing it.

How To Stop It

Kudzu, though hardy, can be harvested using sharp implements. A problem with cutting kudzu is that it can regrow extremely fast, as much as one foot in a single day.

An image of Kudzu leaves and vine

Basic Information

Kudzu can grow to many meters long. It spreads quickly, and can kill large trees off. The best time for harvesting Kudzu is in the winter, because the rate of growing is greatly diminished.
